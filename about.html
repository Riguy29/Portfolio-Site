Background

I grew up in Green Bay, Wisconsin, in the shadow of Lambeau Field. As an only child for the first eight years of my life, I was not thrilled about having to deal with a little sister, but we have only grown closer over the years. In 2016, I was blessed with a half-brother and it has been a wonderful experience to watch him grow up. I have always been a nerd at heart. As a child, you couldn't pull me away from computers and tech. Whether it was disassembling a calculator to get a sneak peek at the circuit board inside or downloading Skyrim's modding tools to investigate how my favorite game worked, I couldn't keep my hands away from tech. I currently reside in Green Bay and live with an adorable little orange cat, Fae.

Education

I took my first computer science class, Fundamentals of Computer Science, during my sophmore year at Southwest Highschool. This class introduced me to some of the most basic aspects of software through the use of the block-based learning program Scratch. As part of the class, we also had a module focused on web developments where we used Adobe Dreamweaver to explore basic HTML,CSS, and Javascript. During my final year, I took the newly introduced Computer Science AP course, in which we dove further into the nitty gritty of computers and learned about algorithms, object-oriented programming, and some basic design patterns. After those first few programming classes I was hooked and I knew I wanted to purse computer science further. My first year in college was spent at Northwestern Technical College, which had a direct transfer program with UWGB. I was able to knock out a good portion of my general education requirements and one or two programming classes. I graduated from the University of Green Bay with a degree in Computer science, with emphasis in both software engineering and cybersecurity

Hobbies

When I'm not working or running errands, you can usually find me catching up on the latest nerd media, tinkering with new technologies, or prepping for my latest Dungeons and Dragons campaign. I was first exposed to D&D in high school when some other students invited me to join them for a one-shot. Although a little nerve-racking at first, I quickly fell in love with it. After numerous sessions as a player, I wanted to try my hand at being the one who makes it all possible, the DM. I bought the DM's guide and got to work. Using a premade campaign setting, I ran my first session and had even more fun than when I was a player. Ever since, I have been consistently running games for friends, coworkers, and anyone who has an inkling of interest in it. I have also expanded into different TTRPG systems such as Call of Cthulu and the Fantasy Flight Star Wars System. I also enjoy spending an afternoon here and huddling up, playing the latest and greatest AAA game, or exploring a cozy indie game. Video games can be some of the most powerful art made, and it's a shame that some people look down on them. Games like Cyberpunk 2077 and Red Dead Redemption have some of the most impactful stories and have brought actual tears to my eyes.
